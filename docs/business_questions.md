# Business Questions

## Purpose

This document defines the key business questions that the Asset-Finance Operations Intelligence System must answer.

The questions are grouped into collections, reconciliation, rider performance, fleet and maintenance, risk, and management decision-making.

## Collections and Repayments

1. How much repayment was expected during each reporting week?
2. How much repayment was actually collected?
3. What was the weekly collection rate?
4. How does collection performance compare across weeks and months?
5. Which riders have unpaid repayment obligations?
6. What is the total outstanding balance across the portfolio?
7. Which riders hold the highest outstanding balances?
8. How much debt was carried forward from previous reporting periods?
9. Which riders have made partial repayments?
10. Which contracts are progressing according to schedule?
11. Which contracts are behind schedule?
12. Which contracts are close to completion?

## Earnings and Payouts

1. How much did each rider earn during the reporting period?
2. What deductions were applied to each rider?
3. How much was deducted for repayments?
4. How much was deducted for maintenance and operational charges?
5. What was the calculated net payout for each rider?
6. Does the calculated payout match the recorded payout?
7. Which riders received zero or negative calculated payouts?
8. Which riders had insufficient earnings to cover their obligations?
9. How have rider earnings changed over time?
10. Which riders show a persistent decline in earnings?

## Reconciliation

1. Which earnings, payment and deduction records match successfully?
2. Which transactions remain unmatched?
3. Are there duplicate transactions or payment references?
4. Are there missing deductions?
5. Are there deductions without a corresponding source record?
6. Which rider statements contain calculation differences?
7. What is the total value of reconciliation variances?
8. Which accounts require manual review?
9. What are the main causes of reconciliation exceptions?
10. How many records were corrected, rejected or flagged for investigation?

## Rider Performance

1. How many riders are active, inactive, suspended or exited?
2. Which riders have missed one or more repayments?
3. Which riders have consecutive missed repayments?
4. Which riders have increasing outstanding balances?
5. Which riders have declining earnings?
6. Which riders have remained inactive for an extended period?
7. Which riders have repeated maintenance-related deductions?
8. Which rider accounts require follow-up?
9. What is the repayment performance by rider?
10. What is the average outstanding balance per rider?

## Fleet and Asset Performance

1. How many assets are active, idle, under maintenance, repossessed or ownership-completed?
2. What is the overall fleet-utilization rate?
3. Which assets have remained idle the longest?
4. Which assets are currently assigned to riders?
5. Are any assets assigned to more than one active rider?
6. Which assets have the highest number of maintenance incidents?
7. Which assets have the highest maintenance cost?
8. What is the average maintenance downtime?
9. Which asset models experience repeated breakdowns?
10. How does utilization vary by asset model, region or status?
11. Which assets are not generating operational activity?
12. Which assets require immediate operational intervention?

## Maintenance and Downtime

1. How many maintenance incidents occurred during the reporting period?
2. What was the total maintenance cost?
3. What was the average maintenance cost per asset?
4. What was the average downtime per maintenance incident?
5. Which maintenance categories occur most frequently?
6. Which assets have repeated maintenance incidents?
7. Which assets have unusually high maintenance costs?
8. How much potential utilization was lost because of downtime?
9. Which maintenance cases remain open?
10. Which assets returned to maintenance shortly after a previous repair?

## Risk and Intervention

1. Which riders should be classified as low, medium, high or critical risk?
2. What factors contributed to each rider's risk classification?
3. Which riders have consecutive missed repayments?
4. Which riders have declining earnings and increasing balances?
5. Which riders have prolonged inactivity?
6. Which riders have repeated maintenance incidents?
7. Which rider accounts require immediate contact?
8. Which accounts should be monitored rather than escalated?
9. What intervention is recommended for each risk level?
10. Has the risk level of each rider improved or worsened over time?

## Management Decision-Making

1. What are the most important changes in collections this week?
2. Which rider accounts contribute the most to outstanding debt?
3. Which reconciliation exceptions have the highest financial impact?
4. Which assets are reducing fleet performance?
5. Which maintenance issues require immediate attention?
6. Which riders require urgent intervention?
7. What operational issues are affecting collections?
8. Where should management focus collection resources?
9. Which portfolio risks are increasing?
10. What actions should management prioritize during the next reporting period?

## Dashboard Alignment

The business questions will be addressed through four main Power BI reporting areas:

### Executive Overview

* Overall collection performance
* Outstanding balances
* Fleet utilization
* Maintenance status
* High-risk rider accounts
* Reconciliation exceptions

### Collections and Reconciliation

* Expected versus actual collections
* Rider balances
* Payout calculations
* Unmatched transactions
* Reconciliation variances

### Fleet and Maintenance

* Asset status
* Fleet utilization
* Idle assets
* Maintenance frequency
* Maintenance costs
* Downtime

### Risk and Intervention

* Rider risk levels
* Risk reasons
* Missed repayments
* Earnings trends
* Outstanding-balance trends
* Recommended interventions
